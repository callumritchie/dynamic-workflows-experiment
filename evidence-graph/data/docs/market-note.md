# Streaming Aggregation: Market Note (synthetic)

A short secondary-research note used to demonstrate doc-source fusion. Numbers here are
illustrative, not real.

## Decision fatigue dominates the viewing session
Across industry panels, the decision of what to watch is the single most cited friction.
The average viewer spends roughly 15-20 minutes choosing, and a large share give up and
default to something familiar rather than choosing a new title. Reducing this decision
cost is the clearest product opportunity.

## Subscription fragmentation and unclear value
Households now pay for four or more services, and content is split across providers, so it
is unclear which subscription a given show is on. Many keep subscriptions they no longer
use because cancelling is friction-laden, inflating the monthly amount paid for unused
services.

## Cross-device continuity is weak
Viewers routinely switch between phone and TV, but watch progress rarely syncs across
devices. Losing your place when resuming on another device is a frequent, low-tolerance
annoyance that erodes trust in the platform.

## Parental controls remain an afterthought
Shared family profiles leak: children reach adult content more often than parents expect.
This is lower frequency but high severity when it occurs.
