P: Oh yeah, so many times. I've probably watched the whole series four times through. But at least I know what I'm getting, you know? It's familiar. It's easy. You don't have to think about it. P: Constantly. I watched like the first episode and a half of some prestige drama—I think it was on HBO? My wife was watching it with me and she wanted to keep going, but I was just not into it. Interviewer: When you're deciding what to watch with your wife versus alone, does that change the process?

P: Oh totally. Alone I'm more likely to just... give up, honestly. Like last Friday I had a free afternoon. I told myself I'd finally watch something good. I went through all these tabs, bookmarked a few things on my phone for later, and then three hours passed and I never actually started anything. I just ended up scrolling on my phone instead, which is pathetic.

Interviewer: Not pathetic. But help me picture that—what does that looking process feel like when you've got three hours free?

P: Stressful, weirdly. Like there's all these choices and I want to pick the *right* one. There's stuff everyone's talking about that I feel like I should watch, there's my wife's list of things she wants us to watch together, there's stuff I added to my list six months ago and forgot about, and then there's the comfort-watch option. P: Yeah, last month I started Breaking Bad again. Which is insane because I've seen it twice. But I was in that stuck place again—couldn't decide—so I just opened it on my laptop while I was working and let it play. At least I knew I'd enjoy it.

Interviewer: How many subscriptions are you paying for right now?

P: God, I don't even know. Netflix, Hulu, Disney+, Max... I think there's an Apple TV+ subscription in there somewhere. Maybe six? Seven? I keep forgetting which ones I have because I don't use most of them.

Interviewer: Do you ever look at all of them together?

P: Not really. I mean the Reelay app is supposed to do that but honestly I mostly just click into Netflix or Max individually when I know what I want to watch. The aggregated view actually makes it worse sometimes because you see everything all mixed up and you realize none of it is appealing.

Interviewer: That's interesting. Can you give me an example?

P: Like this week my roommate was visiting and we wanted to watch a movie together. I opened Reelay to find something, and it just showed me this wall of stuff. Movies, shows, clips, everything. I ended up just going to Netflix directly and browsing their top ten or whatever. At least then the field was narrowed down.

Interviewer: Why did that feel better?

P: Because someone else made a choice for me, I guess? Like Netflix is saying "these are the ten movies people are watching right now." It's a smaller menu. I feel like I'm choosing from a curation rather than from an infinite library.

Interviewer: Do you ever ask friends what they're watching?

P: Sometimes. My brother texted me last week about some show on Apple TV that he's obsessed with, and I actually added it to a list. But then I haven't watched it. And honestly I don't even remember the name of it now. I'd have to text him back and ask.

Interviewer: When your brother told you about it, what made you interested enough to add it to a list?

P: He was really enthusiastic about it. Like he actually described what happens, not just "oh this is good." He made it sound like something I'd specifically like, not just generic hype. That actually made me want to check it out... but then I still haven't.

Interviewer: What's stopping you from actually starting it?

P: I don't know. I'm worried it'll be a slog, or I'll like it but then forget to watch the next episode and lose momentum, or it'll turn out he was wrong about my taste. It's a bigger commitment mentally than just opening The Office.
