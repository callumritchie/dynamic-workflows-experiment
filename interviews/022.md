Interviewer: Tell me about what happened the last time you sat down to watch something. Walk me through that.

P: Oh man, okay so this was like Tuesday night. I got home around 7, had dinner, and I was like "I just want to zone out and watch something." I had about an hour and a half before bed. So I grabbed my iPad and just... opened Netflix first. Scrolled through like 15 minutes of stuff I'd already seen or didn't care about. Then I remembered—I think the new season of The Bear dropped? Or maybe it was Hulu? I wasn't sure. So I closed Netflix and opened Hulu. Didn't see it there. Checked my email to see if I got a notification... nothing. Went back to Netflix. Scrolled some more. My roommate was like "just pick something" but I couldn't decide. I ended up rewatching the first episode of Severance again because I knew I liked it. Which was fine, but I really didn't want to rewatch anything.

Interviewer: Why weren't you sure which service had The Bear?

P: Because I have three subscriptions—Netflix, Hulu, and Disney+ because of a Hulu bundle thing. And I don't really keep track of what's on where. Like, I know some shows are just on one platform, but when a new season drops, I have to hunt. Plus my memory's not great. I'll think a show is on Netflix and it's not anymore because it moved or got pulled. It's annoying.

Interviewer: How often does that hunting happen?

P: A lot. Honestly probably like four or five times a week? Someone will text me "oh you should watch this" or I'll remember a show I was following and I'm like "wait, where was that again?" And then I'm just bouncing between apps. I've definitely given up on shows because I couldn't find them fast enough. Like, I think I wanted to start Daria recently but I couldn't find it anywhere and I just never looked hard enough to figure out which service had it.

Interviewer: And that happens, and then what—you just stop watching?

P: Yeah, or I'll pick something else that's right in front of me. That's why I end up rewatching so much. It's the path of least resistance. If it's already on my Netflix homepage, I'm just going to watch that instead of digging around for something I'm actually excited about. Which is stupid, right? Like, I'm paying for all these services. I should be able to just... I don't know, ask where something is.

Interviewer: Talk about the last time you actively decided to try a new show—not a rewatch. What was that like?

P: Okay, so like two weeks ago, Friday night. I texted my friend group asking for recommendations because I was in the mood for a specific vibe—something funny but not a comedy? Like a dramedy, I guess. And one friend was like "watch Shrinking." So I knew what to watch. But then I had to search for it. I opened Netflix—not there. Opened Hulu—bingo. Great. But I lost like ten minutes of my evening just doing that search. And I remember thinking "I don't have time for this, I just want to start watching." 

If I had to do that search process on, like, a Sunday morning with no time pressure, whatever. But when I'm tired and just want to fall asleep to something? Those ten minutes feel like an hour.

Interviewer: How does that play out across your devices? You mentioned iPad.

P: Yeah, so I watch on my iPad mostly at night, my TV when I'm with my roommate, and sometimes my phone if I'm on the couch but the TV's being used. And honestly, that's another pain—like, sometimes I'll start a show on my iPad and then want to move to the TV, and I have to hunt for where I left off. I think one of my apps will remember, but I'm never sure which one. Last week I started Season 2 of Shrinking on my iPad and then when I went to the TV I couldn't remember if I was five episodes in or six episodes in. I just guessed and skipped around until the scenes looked familiar.

Interviewer: That sounds like a lot of friction just to watch something.

P: It is. And I'm someone who watches a lot, so you'd think I'd have this figured out. But the more shows I'm following, the more confusing it gets. Like right now I'm supposedly watching... I want to say six shows? But I don't know where I am in all of them, and I don't know which services have them. I have to think way too hard about it.

Interviewer: If you could redesign that whole experience—from deciding what to watch to actually pressing play—what would change?

P: Oh, I'd want everything in one place. Like, all my subscriptions, all the shows, everything. I'd open one app and I'd just see "here's what's new, here's where you left off, here's what you're in the middle of." And it would just... know. I wouldn't have to bounce between four apps. I could search once and it would tell me which service has it. And it would actually remember where I left off on every screen.
