Interviewer: Tell me about the last time you decided to watch something. Walk me through what happened.

P: Oh man, okay. So it was like Tuesday night, maybe 9:30? I had just finished eating and my partner had gone to bed early. I had like an hour before I wanted to wind down, and I just... opened Netflix. And I scrolled. I think I scrolled for like 15 minutes and couldn't find anything. Everything looked either too heavy or I'd already seen half of it.

Interviewer: What do you mean by "too heavy"?

P: Like, I wasn't in the mood for something that required a lot of thinking. I wanted something I could just turn my brain off to, but nothing felt right. So I ended up just... putting on The Office again. Like, the same episode I've watched probably 30 times. It's comfortable, right? I know exactly what's going to happen.

Interviewer: Is that something you do a lot?

P: Yeah, probably more than I'd like to admit. I'd say like three, four times a week I'll just open the app and end up rewatching something. It's frustrating because I'm literally paying for all these services and I'm not using them. But in the moment it just feels easier than spending 20 minutes deciding. My brain's already tired from work.

Interviewer: You mentioned paying for multiple services. How many subscriptions do you have right now?

P: Netflix, Prime Video, Disney Plus, and I think HBO Max is still active? Maybe Hulu too, I can't remember. My mom keeps saying I should cancel some of them but honestly I don't know what's on where. Like, I don't know if something I want to watch is on Prime or HBO. So I just bounce around.

Interviewer: You bounce between apps?

P: Yeah. Like last weekend I wanted to watch something specific—I can't even remember what show now—and I thought it was on Netflix but it wasn't. So I opened Prime and scrolled there. Then I think I checked Disney. By the time I'd checked all three I'd just given up and watched Friends reruns on Netflix again.

Interviewer: So you knew what you wanted to watch but not where it was?

P: Exactly. And it's not like I could just Google it super easily in the moment because then I'd have to leave the app and come back. Well, I could, but I don't know. It just kills the mood. I end up just defaulting to whatever I've already seen.

Interviewer: What time of day does this usually happen?

P: Evenings mostly. Like after dinner, when I want to relax. And like Saturday mornings sometimes when I'm having coffee before doing anything else. Those are my watch times.

Interviewer: Have you ever, say, made a list of things you want to watch?

P: In my head, yeah. Like I'll remember "oh, someone recommended that show" or I'll see something trending on Twitter. But by the time I get to the app I've forgotten what it was, or it doesn't sound as good anymore. I don't write things down. That feels like too much effort.

Interviewer: Talk me through what happens when you do remember something.

P: Okay so like, my friend mentioned this show—I think it was Severance? A few weeks ago. And I was excited about it. I went to Netflix and started typing it in and... I don't know, it just didn't feel like the right mood that night. So I skipped it. And then I never got back to it. Now I don't remember if it's still something I wanted to watch or not.

Interviewer: Why didn't it feel like the right mood?

P: I think it looked kind of dark? Like the description seemed serious and I was just wanting something lighter. But I could have been wrong. I don't know. I didn't really give it a fair shot.

Interviewer: Do you use your phone or TV mostly to watch?

P: TV usually, but sometimes I'll open Netflix on my phone if I'm in bed or something. My phone is fine for scrolling through stuff, but the actual watching is always on the TV. Like if I'm on the couch I'll have my phone in my hand while the TV is on but I'm looking at Twitter or whatever.

Interviewer: Are you making the decision on your phone or on the TV?

P: Oh, the phone usually. I'll scroll through my phone and then like, decide what to watch, and send it to the TV. Or I'll already have the app open on the TV and scroll there. Actually that's probably worse because the scrolling is slower on the TV.

Interviewer: Worse in what way?

P: Like it's clunkier, more scrolling, feels more annoying. So I end up giving up faster. On my phone at least I can scroll quickly. But then the decision paralysis is the same.

Interviewer: When you do finally pick something, how often do you finish it?

P: Oof. Honestly not that much. I'd say I finish like 50% of what I start? Some shows just aren't as good as the hype. Or they're slow at the beginning. I don't have patience to push through a slow first episode anymore.

Interviewer: That matters for what you're paying for these services?

P: I mean... not really? I'm paying anyway. But yeah, it's like I'm wasting money. I could probably cut back to like two subscriptions if I was actually watching stuff consistently instead of just rewatching The Office for the hundredth time.

Interviewer: What would it take for you to feel like you were actually using them well?

P: Honestly, if I could just... see everything in one place, maybe? Like if I didn't have to think about which app to open, and I could just see what's available? I don't know. Maybe that would help. Right now it just feels too fragmented.
