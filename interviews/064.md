Interviewer: Let's start with last week—what did you actually watch?

P: Oh man, it was all over the place. I started this new thriller on Netflix Monday night, then Tuesday I was looking for something lighter because my partner had people over, and I just... I ended up scrolling for like twenty minutes. We eventually watched some movie I'd already seen before. I know that's not what we wanted to do, but it was faster than deciding.

Interviewer: Walk me through that decision process. What were you actually looking for that night?

P: Something funny but not too dumb, you know? Something we could have on while talking. I *thought* we had some comedy special on one of our services, but I genuinely couldn't remember if it was Netflix or if I'd seen it on a friend's account. So I just gave up and picked something I knew was there.

Interviewer: How often does that happen—you can't remember which service has what?

P: More than I'd like to admit. Honestly, probably two or three times a week I'm like, "Wait, is this on Disney+ or Hulu?" And I'm paying for both, so it's annoying. I usually just pick whatever I open first.

Interviewer: What made you pick Netflix first that night versus Hulu?

P: It was already open on the TV. Lazy, I guess. But also I think my family watched it last, so it was fresh in my mind. If I had to cold-start and pick the app, I probably would've spent another five minutes deciding which one to check first.

Interviewer: Tell me about your subscriptions. How many do you actually have right now?

P: Five. Netflix, Hulu, Disney+, Max, and Amazon Prime Video. I rotate through them a bit, honestly. I'm thinking about dropping one soon because I'm not keeping up. Like, I signed up for Apple TV+ because my friend wouldn't stop talking about Severance, but I watched two episodes and it's been sitting there for two months. I'm paying for something I'm not using.

Interviewer: What made you not go back to it?

P: I forgot about it, mostly. And when I remember it exists, there are like five other shows already on my radar that I'm more excited about. So by the time I think, "Oh right, Severance," I'm already deep into something else on Netflix.

Interviewer: Can you think of a time recently where you found out about something you would've actually wanted to watch, but by the time you found it, you'd already moved on?

P: Yeah, last month my coworker was obsessed with this show—I forget the name now, some science fiction thing. She kept telling me I'd love it. So like a week later I'm looking around trying to find it, and I have no idea which streaming service it's on. I could've Googled it, but I was tired, so I just went back to the episode of The Bear I was already watching. I never went back to look for her show.

Interviewer: How did you feel about that? Just accepting you wouldn't watch it?

P: It's annoying, but also... normal? Like, there's so much to watch that it doesn't feel like a huge loss. If it came up again organically—like if she mentioned it at lunch—maybe I'd actually try to find it then. But I'm not going to spend my own time hunting for something I'm not even sure exists on my accounts.

Interviewer: What's your actual process when you *do* decide what to watch?

P: I usually open one app and scroll the recommendations for a bit. If nothing grabs me, I'll open another app. If I'm still not feeling anything after twenty minutes, I just pick something familiar or turn on the news. I don't really have a system.

Interviewer: Does the bill matter to you? Like, do you think about how much you're paying when you're scrolling?

P: Definitely. I recently did the math and realized I was spending like eighty-five dollars a month on streaming. That's a thousand a year. It's ridiculous. So I've been like, "Why am I paying for five if I'm only really using two or three?" But then the problem is I don't know which ones I'm *actually* using because my family uses them too.

Interviewer: How would you even figure that out?

P: Good question. I don't think there's an easy way. I'd have to like, track it manually for a month? Or just log in to each one and see what was watched recently. It's too much friction.

Interviewer: If you could fix one thing about how you watch TV, what would it be?

P: Honestly? Just knowing what I have access to without opening five apps. Like, there's a moment where I want to watch something and it would be great to just see, "Oh, this is on Netflix," without having to search three different apps or Google it. Save me the guessing.

Interviewer: What would actually get you to use a service like that?

P: If it worked. Like if it was actually fast and didn't feel like one more clunky thing to manage. I'm already tired.
