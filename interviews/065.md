Interviewer: How have you been using streaming this past week or so?

P: Oh man, honestly it's been kind of chaotic. Tuesday night my sister called from Portland and asked if I wanted to watch something together over FaceTime. We were both free, so I said sure. But then I had to go digging through my apps—I've got Netflix, Hulu, the Disney+ bundle, HBO Max, and I just added Peacock for the Olympics coverage. I was switching between like five different apps trying to figure out where something we'd both like was actually available. It took forever.

Interviewer: How long did that actually take?

P: I was probably on my phone for twenty minutes, maybe longer. My sister was just sitting there waiting, which felt bad. We eventually just threw on The Great British Bake Off because I know that's on Netflix, but honestly neither of us was really excited about it. We both would've preferred something else but I just... gave up looking.

Interviewer: What were you hoping to find instead?

P: I don't know, something newer maybe? A movie we hadn't seen? There's so much stuff out there but somehow you still end up watching the same things. My roommate's been going on about this true crime series everyone's talking about, and I have no idea which app it's on. I keep meaning to search for it but then I just watch whatever's on my home screen.

Interviewer: Tell me about how you're paying for all of these right now.

P: Yeah, so I'm on my parents' Netflix account, which is technically not allowed anymore, but we've been doing it forever. My mom just pays for that one. Then I'm splitting Hulu with my roommate—we split it by Venmo, I think it's like eight bucks a month or something? HBO Max I pay for myself because I wanted to watch Succession when it was airing. And then the Disney bundle came as a deal when I signed up for something else. Peacock I'm paying for but honestly I'm not even sure how much that costs.

Interviewer: So you're not sure what you're actually paying every month?

P: Not really, no. I mean, I know the Hulu thing is shared, and I pay the Disney bundle, but beyond that... it's somewhere on my credit card. My mom yelled at me last month because the Netflix price went up again, and I was like, "Yeah, I noticed." It's ridiculous. I definitely use Netflix, but I'm not sure I even open Peacock anymore. I signed up for the Olympics and now I'm just paying for it.

Interviewer: When you do sit down to watch something, walk me through that decision-making process.

P: Usually it's evening or weekend. I'll be on the couch, I'm bored, and I'm like okay, what do I want to watch? And I'll open Netflix first because that's just habit. I'll scroll through for a few minutes, see what's new. If nothing jumps out, I'll check Hulu. My roommate's always like, "Did you check HBO?" because she has better taste than me in shows, and she'll be like, "This thing just came out and it's so good." Then I have to go check that. By the time I've checked like three apps, I've lost twenty minutes and I'm frustrated.

Interviewer: Has that happened recently?

P: Yeah, last Saturday I had friends coming over and I wanted to have something on in the background. I spent like twenty-five minutes jumping between apps looking for something light and funny, and they were already there, so it was awkward. I just ended up putting on The Office again because I know where it is. They didn't care, but I felt like I was unprepared, you know?

Interviewer: So when your sister called to watch together, that felt different than watching alone?

P: Oh totally. Like, I'm fine sitting alone and scrolling a bit. But when someone's waiting for me or I'm trying to make a plan with someone, that's when it gets annoying. My brother lives in Denver and we used to text about shows we were watching, but lately it's just been harder to coordinate because we have to figure out what we both have access to. He's got different subscriptions than me. Last month he wanted to watch something with my family on a group call and we couldn't all agree on anything we could all actually watch.

Interviewer: How often does that kind of watching-together thing happen?

P: Maybe like twice a month? Sometimes once a month. It's usually my sister or my brother, or occasionally my roommate and I will have a friend over. It doesn't happen super regularly, but when it does I want it to actually work, you know? I don't want to spend the first half hour troubleshooting.

Interviewer: Is there anything else that's been frustrating about streaming lately?

P: Honestly, the price thing just feels sneaky. Like, I don't pay enough attention to when things go up. I just noticed the other day that I'm probably paying like 60 bucks a month across everything, and I'm not even sure what I'm using. I don't think I've opened Peacock in six weeks. I should probably cancel it, but I'd have to go find my password or whatever. It's just... easier not to.

Interviewer: What would make this easier?

P: I don't know. Maybe just seeing everything in one place? Like, what if I could just see all my subscriptions and what's available, instead of opening six different apps when I want to watch something with someone?
