Interviewer: Thanks for taking the time to chat with us. I wanted to understand how you're deciding what to watch these days. Walk me through what happened the last time you sat down to watch something.

P: Oh man, yeah. So this was like... Tuesday night? Wednesday? One of those. My partner was in the mood for something. We had maybe 45 minutes before we both got tired, and I was like, "I'll find something good." I opened my phone — I have Reelay on there — and just started scrolling. I must have spent, I don't know, 20 minutes just flipping through stuff. Nothing grabbed me.

Interviewer: What were you looking for in those 20 minutes?

P: Honestly, something we hadn't seen before, but also something that wouldn't require too much brain power. Like, we didn't want to start season one of a heavy drama. But every time I'd see something that looked interesting, I'd either think, "Oh wait, I'm not sure if we have that," or I'd click on it and it would open Netflix in my head before I remembered we don't have Netflix. We have the Disney bundle and HBO and one other thing.

Interviewer: So you ended up not watching anything?

P: No, we put on The Office. Which we've seen three times. [laughs] It's always there, it's always available, and I didn't have to think about it. My partner was annoyed, but I was like, "I don't have the energy to figure this out right now."

Interviewer: When you opened Reelay and started scrolling, what made you stop?

P: I just... gave up. It felt endless. And even when I'd find something that looked decent, I'd have this moment of like, "Is this on one of our services?" And I didn't want to spend another five minutes checking. So I just picked what I knew worked.

Interviewer: That happens to you a lot?

P: More than I'd like to admit, yeah. Probably once a week, at least. Though some nights it's easier. Like, if I'm alone and I just want to put something on in the background while I'm doing dishes, I'm fine with whatever. But when my partner is sitting down and we're actually making it a thing, I feel this pressure to find something *good*, and that's when I freeze up.

Interviewer: You mentioned not knowing which services have which shows. Is that something that comes up a lot?

P: Yeah, constantly. Like, last week my brother mentioned this show — I think it was on Apple? — and I got excited about it, but then when I went to find it on my phone, I had no idea where to look. I clicked around a bit and I think I ended up just going to Google on the side to figure out if I even had it. Then I realized I didn't, and I kind of forgot about the whole thing.

Interviewer: How often do you watch from your phone versus your TV?

P: Oh, that's another thing. Usually we watch on the TV, but sometimes I'll scroll on my phone to find something while my partner is already on the couch. So I'll be like, "I found something on..." — and then I have to remember: is that showing on the TV right now? Like, do I have it queued up? Because sometimes I'll find something on the app on my phone, get excited, go over to the TV, and then the TV isn't showing what I picked. I have to go back to my phone, look it up again. It's annoying.

Interviewer: Does that happen regularly?

P: Yeah, more than once a month for sure. There's this disconnect where the app on my phone feels separate from what I'm actually watching. Like, they should be the same thing, but they don't feel connected.

Interviewer: When you do find something you want to watch, what's that moment like?

P: It's rare [laughs], but when it happens, it's usually because I see a cover or a title that just immediately makes me think, "Oh, I want to watch that." Like, two weeks ago I saw something about a survival show — it looked interesting, visually stood out — and I was like, okay, I'm doing this one. That took maybe 30 seconds. No thinking.

Interviewer: So the friction isn't always there.

P: No, it's just when I have to choose. When I'm actively shopping, it gets hard. If someone recommends something specific, I'm usually fine with it. But if it's on me to come up with the idea and figure out what we have access to? That's the hard part.

Interviewer: Is there anything else that gets in the way?

P: I guess I've also just kind of given up on trying new things. Like, I have subscriptions I'm paying for, but I feel like I only watch maybe 30 percent of what's actually available to me. Most of the time I'm just cycling through the shows I already know and like. I don't even try to discover stuff anymore because it takes too long.

Interviewer: When was the last time you actually discovered something new?

P: Honestly, I can't remember. Probably a friend told me about it, or it went viral on Twitter or something. I don't discover things through the apps themselves anymore.
