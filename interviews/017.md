Interviewer: Tell me about how your week of watching TV has gone. What have you actually sat down to watch in the last few days?

P: Oh man, it's been all over the place. I finally finished the last season of *The Boys* on Thursday night after the kids went to bed, which was great because I've been invested in that for like two years. But I started something new on Tuesday that I can't even remember the name of—something my wife showed me. It was fine, but I don't think I'm going back to it.

Interviewer: How did you find that show your wife showed you?

P: She just pulled it up on the TV while we were eating dinner. I think it was on Hulu? Or maybe Disney+, I'm not totally sure. She had the remote and was flipping through, and she's like, "Oh, this one's supposed to be good," so we tried it. But then I sat down yesterday morning on my iPad to actually watch something, and I spent like twenty minutes just... scrolling. Scrolling through I don't even know what. I probably went through four different apps. Netflix, then Prime because I remembered I have something saved there, then back to Netflix, then to the Disney app.

Interviewer: What were you looking for in that twenty minutes?

P: Something easy? I had maybe thirty minutes before I needed to shower and head to work. I didn't want to start something new that I'd have to think about. Maybe a show I'm already watching or something short. But when I opened Netflix, I literally scrolled past the same ten rows of stuff I've already seen. I know *The Boys* is done, I know I finished *Breaking Bad* months ago, so why do they keep showing me that? And then I got frustrated and just... closed it.

Interviewer: What did you end up doing instead?

P: Honestly, I listened to a podcast. Which is great, but I *wanted* to watch something. It's just easier to not.

Interviewer: You mentioned saving things on Prime—do you actively keep a watchlist or does that just happen?

P: Both? I save stuff when I remember to, but I'm not great about it. When I find something I want to watch, I add it somewhere. Or I think I add it. Half the time I get to the next day and I'm not sure if I actually saved it or just thought about saving it. There's definitely shows I've wanted to watch that I can't find anymore because I didn't write them down.

Interviewer: That's frustrating. Let me ask about another angle—you have subscriptions to multiple services, right?

P: Yeah, we have Netflix, Hulu, Disney+, HBO Max, and Prime. Sometimes I wonder if we actually use all of them. My wife pays for Disney+ because she likes their shows, and we basically just split everything else. But I'll be honest, I have no idea what's actually available on some of them. Like, I couldn't tell you what's new on Hulu this week without digging around.

Interviewer: When something new drops that you care about, how do you find out about it?

P: Twitter, mostly. Or my friends text me like, "Did you see *House of the Dragon* is back?" That's usually how I know. That's actually how I found out the last season of *The Boys* was available—someone posted about it. Otherwise I'd probably miss it for weeks.

Interviewer: Has that ever happened? Missing something because you didn't know it was there?

P: Yeah, absolutely. I found out like three weeks after it aired that something I wanted to watch was already available. At that point, half the internet has already spoiled it, so what's the point?

Interviewer: Tell me about the device side of things. How are you watching these different services?

P: Mainly the TV in the living room, but I also watch on my iPad when I'm in bed or traveling. My phone sometimes, but the screen's too small to commit to anything real. The annoying thing is when I'm in the middle of something on one device and then switch to another, I have to remember exactly where I was. Like, I watched two episodes of *The Boys* on my iPad, and then when I went back to the TV, I couldn't for the life of me remember if I'd seen episode seven or eight.

Interviewer: How'd you figure that out?

P: I just rewound and skipped ahead until I found the one I recognized. Not ideal. I think most apps remember where you are, but I'm not sure they all sync properly across devices for me.

Interviewer: One more thing—you mentioned you have kids. How does that play into what you're watching?

P: Oh, that's a whole thing. We have a kids profile set up on Netflix and Disney+, right? But my son somehow got into my profile last week and started watching some adult stuff—I don't even know how because we thought we had it password protected. My wife found him halfway through something definitely not meant for kids. So now we're paranoid about the iPad just sitting there.

Interviewer: How often does that happen?

P: That was the first real incident, but now we're just more careful. We basically don't leave the iPad out anymore, which is annoying because it was nice for him to have access to his shows on his own.

Interviewer: Makes sense. If you could change how you watch shows—across all your different apps and devices—what's the one thing that would actually make your life easier?

P: I just want to know what I'm supposed to be watching and actually be able to get to it without digging around. And I want my wife to be able to watch her stuff without me accidentally messing it up or my kid getting into something.
