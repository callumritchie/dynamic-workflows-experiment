Interviewer: So I want to understand how you're deciding what to watch these days. Can you walk me through what happened last night when you sat down to watch something?

P: Oh man, last night was a whole thing. The kids finally went to bed around 8:30, and I had maybe forty minutes before I'd just collapse. I wanted to actually watch something for adults, you know? So I'm scrolling through Netflix, and I see this show I've been meaning to watch—I think it's "The Bear"? But then I'm like, wait, is that on Netflix or is that on Hulu? I feel like I remember hearing it was on something else.

Interviewer: What did you do?

P: I just... I texted my husband. He was downstairs. I was like, "Hey, is The Bear on Netflix?" And he didn't know either. So I just gave up and watched The Office again. Which is fine, it's comfortable, but it's not what I wanted to do. I had forty minutes of free time and I spent fifteen of it just trying to figure out what I could actually watch.

Interviewer: Has this happened before?

P: All the time. Like, my mom will call and be like, "Did you see that new season of [something]?" and I'll be like, "Is that still on?" Because I genuinely don't remember if we even have that service anymore. We've had so many subscriptions over the years, and then I'll cancel one, and then my husband signs up for another one, and I never really know what we're paying for.

Interviewer: What services do you have right now?

P: Netflix, definitely. Hulu. Disney Plus because of the kids. Amazon Prime, though honestly I forget that's streaming too—I just think of it as the Amazon thing. And then... I think we might still have HBO Max? My brother was using it or something. I'd have to check. It's honestly embarrassing how little I know about what we're paying for.

Interviewer: When you do sit down to watch, what's usually on your mind?

P: It depends on the day. On weekends, I'm looking for something the whole family can watch together, so it's like, "Okay, is there anything animated, nothing scary, nothing with too much bad language?" That's usually a half-hour conversation in itself. Then on weeknights when the kids are asleep, I want something smart but not heavy—I don't want to think too hard because I'm exhausted. And I never watch a full episode. I watch like thirty minutes and fall asleep.

Interviewer: So when you're looking for something to watch, how do you actually find it?

P: I just open Netflix and scroll. For like ten minutes. Looking at the same stuff. And if I don't immediately see something that grabs me, I give up. My husband is better at this—he'll like, remember shows he wants to watch and will search for them. I just... I can't hold that information in my head. I'll see a commercial for something, think "Oh that looks good," and then two weeks later I've completely forgotten what it was.

Interviewer: Has there been anything recently that you actually wanted to watch but couldn't find?

P: My coworker was talking about "Severance." She was raving about it, and I was like, "Oh, I'll check it out." So on Tuesday night I opened Netflix, looked for it, and it wasn't there. I thought maybe she meant Hulu? So I opened Hulu, looked, and I'm pretty sure I didn't see it. So I just assumed it was on one of the other ones I don't have, and I dropped it. I didn't try hard enough to find it.

Interviewer: Do you think you'd have watched it if you knew which service it was on?

P: Definitely. If I could just see a list like, "Here's what I can watch right now," I would just pick from that. Instead I'm just bouncing around, and it feels like I'm missing everything. The irony is I'm paying for all of this and I'm watching repeats of The Office.

Interviewer: How often do you actually open each service?

P: Netflix is like, open constantly. Hulu sometimes. Disney Plus I open when the kids want to watch something. I basically never open the others. Which is probably why I forget I have them. And then I get the bill and I'm like, "Oh right, that's still active."

Interviewer: What would make this easier?

P: I don't know. Like, actually knowing what I have? And being able to search for a show and just see where it is? That would be huge. Right now I'm just guessing. And I'm too tired to care enough to solve it, so I watch The Office instead.
