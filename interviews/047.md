Interviewer: So tell me about the last time you started watching something new. What was that?

P: Oh god, like three days ago. I was just doom-scrolling on my phone in bed, and I saw this post about *The Diplomat* season two coming back — I'd forgotten it even had a season two coming. So I opened Netflix on my phone, started the first episode, got like twenty minutes in, and then my roommate was like "come watch something in the living room."

Interviewer: What made you switch?

P: Well, she had her boyfriend over and they wanted to actually hang out, you know? So I went out there and we started *Love on the Spectrum U.S.* on the big TV. Got through maybe two episodes of that. But then later that night, I was back in my room and thought, okay, I want to keep going with *Diplomat* since I'd already started it. And I literally could not remember where I left off. Like, was it 20 minutes? 30? Did I watch a full episode? I ended up just restarting it, and I wasn't even sure if I'd already seen the beginning or not.

Interviewer: That sounds frustrating. Do you remember how you were searching for it?

P: Yeah, I went to Netflix on my phone and just scrolled through "Continue Watching," but honestly the thumbnails are so small that I wasn't confident I was looking at the right thing. And then the next day I went to the TV and started Netflix there, and the "Continue Watching" row was totally different — it was showing me stuff from like a week ago that someone else in the apartment had watched. So I kind of had to hunt for *Diplomat* again.

Interviewer: Multiple people use Netflix on that TV?

P: Yeah, so does everyone. My roommate, her boyfriend sometimes, my other roommate's girlfriend. It's chaotic. So the Continue Watching is basically useless to me on the main TV. On my phone it's better, but even then it's mixed in with stuff I watched months ago.

Interviewer: How do you usually decide what to watch when you sit down?

P: Honestly? I scroll. I have like four different apps — Netflix, Max, Disney+, I think Hulu still? — and when I can't decide, I just scroll through one until something grabs me. But half the time I either see something I've already watched and forgot about, or I open something and then realize I don't have the right subscription.

Interviewer: That second thing — tell me more about that.

P: Like, last week I was in the mood for something specific, and I remembered seeing a recommendation for *Slow Horses* somewhere. Sounded good, so I opened Max and searched for it, and... it's not there anymore, I guess? Or maybe I was thinking of Apple TV+. So I wasted like five minutes jumping between apps trying to find it. Eventually I found it on Apple TV+ but then I had to actually think about whether I still wanted to subscribe to another thing, and honestly I just gave up.

Interviewer: You didn't watch it.

P: No. By then I'd scrolled so much I just opened Netflix again and rewatched part of something I'd already seen because it was easy.

Interviewer: Why was that easier?

P: Because I knew it was there, I knew I had it, and I didn't have to think. But also kind of annoying because I could have watched something new.

Interviewer: Let's zoom out a bit. How much are you watching right now? Like, how many hours a week would you say?

P: Oh man. Probably more than I should. Maybe three hours on a weeknight if I get home early, five or six on weekends? I'm very much a binge person. Like, if a show hooks me, I will blow through it in a few days. I watched *Baby Reindeer* in like two sittings.

Interviewer: Do you ever watch with other people?

P: Sometimes. My roommate and I try to have a show we watch together, but it's hard because of the schedule thing — she works nights sometimes, I'm in the office weird hours. And then my best friend lives like four states away, and we text about shows constantly. Like, she'll send me screenshots or clips and be like "watch this," and I'll be like "okay, adding to the list," but then we never actually sync up to talk about it while watching. I'll watch it and then she'll watch it two weeks later, and it's like... we didn't really watch it together, you know? It's just more content I'm consuming alone.

Interviewer: Would you want to watch with her while she's four states away? Like, what would that even look like?

P: I don't know, honestly. We have FaceTime, but it's awkward to be on the phone the whole time. We'd have to start at the same time, I guess? And then what, just call each other and watch in silence? That sounds weird. But also, I hate that she finishes stuff before I do and then texts me spoilers by accident, or I spoil her. There's just no good way to actually sync up without, like, making it a weird scheduled thing.

Interviewer: How many subscriptions are you paying for right now?

P: I think five? Netflix, Max, Disney+... and I share Apple TV+ with someone. And then my parents pay for Hulu, which I can access. I think that's it? Maybe Prime Video? I genuinely don't know how many I'm paying for versus which ones I'm actively using. It's kind of a mess.

Interviewer: Do you ever cancel any?

P: Not really. I mean, I should, but then you cancel something and two weeks later you remember a show you wanted to watch and it's only on that service. So I just keep them all.
