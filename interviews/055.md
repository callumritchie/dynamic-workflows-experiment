Interviewer: Can you walk me through what happened last night when you were trying to find something to watch?

P: Oh man, yeah. So my sister called around 8 PM—she's in Portland, I'm in Denver—and we wanted to do one of those watch-together things. We've been trying to sync up on something for like two weeks. And I was like, "okay, let me see what we have," and I literally spent... I don't know, 20 minutes? Just opening apps. Netflix, then Disney+, then Max, then Hulu. My sister's got a different combination of subscriptions than I do, so I'm trying to remember what she has while I'm looking at what I have.

Interviewer: What were you looking for, or was it just browsing?

P: We wanted something not too heavy. Like, we're both tired, neither of us wanted to start a true crime thing or anything depressing. So I'm thinking comedies, maybe something we could finish in like two episodes. And I'd open Netflix, scroll for a bit, then I'd be like, "oh, but does she have this?" and I'd have to ask her, and she'd say no, and then I'd go back to browsing, and it was just... exhausting, honestly. I ended up saying forget it, and we just didn't watch anything. We talked on the phone instead.

Interviewer: Has this happened before—where you gave up?

P: Yeah, more than I'd like to admit. Last month my brother wanted to watch this nature documentary together. I think it was on Apple TV? Or maybe Netflix in some countries? I couldn't figure out if we could actually both stream it legally or if one of us didn't have it, so I just said, "eh, let's do something else," and we watched this random comedy special instead. Which was fine, but it wasn't what we wanted.

Interviewer: When you eventually do find something, how do you remember where it is next time?

P: I don't, really. Like, there's this show—I think it's called *The Diplomat*—my mom mentioned it's good, and I *know* I saw it on one of my apps a while back, but I have no idea which one. So two weeks ago I'm looking for something to watch with my girlfriend, and I'm like, "oh, what about that show my mom mentioned?" and I have to search for it on every single service to find where it actually is. It's on Netflix, it turns out, but I just wasted time looking.

Interviewer: How often are you doing that—searching across apps like that?

P: Probably two or three times a week? And most of the time I find what I'm looking for, eventually, but there's always this moment where I'm like, "is this on the one I already pay for, or do I need to add another subscription?" And I don't love spending money, so I'll be like, "maybe I'll just wait until I borrow someone's password." Which I know is not... ideal, but when you're already frustrated, it's tempting.

Interviewer: You mentioned borrowing passwords. How does that usually work?

P: My cousin shares her Disney+ with me, I share my Hulu with her. My parents technically aren't supposed to have my Netflix password but they do. It's just this weird system where if I really want to watch something and I don't have it, I'll text someone. But then there's always that awkward moment of asking, or feeling bad because I'm adding another person to someone else's account.

Interviewer: So how do you and your sister usually end up picking something when you do manage to watch together?

P: We'll usually go with something one of us has already started, or something that's been recommended by someone we trust. Like, my sister texted me last week saying *The Bear* was incredible, and I'm like, "oh, is that on your subscription?" and she said yes, so I went and added it to my own list. But I still haven't actually watched it because I keep forgetting which app it's on. I think it's Hulu? Or was it... I don't know.

Interviewer: When you do finally sit down with your sister or your girlfriend to watch something together, does one person control the remote, or how does that work?

P: Usually whoever's on their own account, we just use that app. So like, last weekend my girlfriend and I watched something on her Netflix account on my TV. It's simple but also kind of silly because I'm paying for Netflix too, so I was just using hers instead of mine.

Interviewer: If you could change one thing about how this whole process works, what would it be?

P: Honestly, I just want to know what I have access to without having to open five different apps and scroll through each one. Like, there's got to be a faster way to see everything at once and figure out what works for everyone I want to watch with. Right now it feels like I'm doing more work to find something than actually watching it.
