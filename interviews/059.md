Interviewer: Thanks for making time. I want to understand how you're thinking about what to watch these days. Can you walk me through what happened the last time you sat down to find something?

Nothing grabbed us. Then I remembered that new season of The Bear dropped on Hulu, so I opened Hulu. We watched two episodes. Pretty standard night.

Interviewer: When you went to Netflix first and scrolled around, what were you actually looking for? What would've made you stop and say "yes, let's watch this"?

P: Something we could both get into, so nothing too dark or heavy, but also not completely vapid. Usually it's like, I see a title I recognize or maybe there's decent cover art and a hook in the description. I'm not going to read a whole synopsis. If I can get the vibe in like 15 seconds, that's what matters.

Interviewer: Got it. And when that didn't happen on Netflix, did you know The Bear was on Hulu, or did you remember it?

P: I remembered it. My mom actually texted me a screenshot of it. But here's the thing—I had to think for a second about whether I even have Hulu active right now. Like, I wasn't sure if I'd kept the subscription or if I'd cancelled it. I logged in and it worked, so apparently I still do, but I honestly don't track it anymore.

Interviewer: You don't track it? What do you mean?

P: Like, I have a lot of these services. I've got Netflix, Hulu, Disney+, Max, Paramount+. I think I also still have Peacock? I'm not 100% sure. My credit card just gets hit every month and I don't really look at the charges. I know it's adding up—it's gotta be like $80 or $90 a month now—but I can never remember which ones I'm actually using without checking. Last month I was thinking about cutting back, so I went through my statements from like six months ago to see what I'd watched on each platform, but it was just a mess. Different card, different statements, some charges had weird names.

Interviewer: Why do you have all of them if you're thinking about cutting back?

P: Because every time there's a show I want to watch, I don't have it. My friend was talking about Severance last year and I thought I had Max, but apparently I'd cancelled it. So I resubscribed. Then I watched like two episodes and nothing else grabbed me. I don't know if I cancelled it again. This actually happened with Peacock too—I feel like I signed up for something specific and then just... never thought about it again.

Interviewer: Do you remember what the something specific was?

P: Honestly, no. Maybe the Olympics? Or a football game? It was probably a one-time thing but I think I just left it running.

Interviewer: So you're reacting to things your friends mention, or shows you hear about, rather than exploring what you already have access to.

P: Yeah, pretty much. I'm not proactive about it. Like, I know there's tons of stuff on these apps I haven't seen, but I don't go hunting. I just know that whatever I'm in the mood for, I probably have it on one of them, so I'll find it. The annoying part is remembering which one. I've definitely gone to Netflix looking for something, spent five minutes searching, then asked my partner "wait, is this on Max?" and had to jump over there.

Interviewer: Has there ever been a time where you actually removed one of the subscriptions on purpose?

P: I think I cancelled something once? Like intentionally? I remember trying to do it and it was weirdly hard. I called to cancel something—maybe Paramount? Interviewer: Do you use that lower tier?

P: I haven't watched anything on it in probably two months. I kept it because cancelling felt like effort and I figured eventually I'd use it. Plus if you cancel and then want to come back, you have to like, re-enter your payment info and it's just annoying.

Interviewer: What would actually make you cancel and stay cancelled?

P: I don't know. If it was like, automatic? Like if I could see that I hadn't opened the app in 60 days and it just... asked me if I wanted to keep paying? Interviewer: Last question: when you do want to figure out what to watch, what's the first thing you do?

P: Usually text someone and ask what they're watching. Or scroll my apps for a few minutes. If nothing jumps out in like two rounds, I'll probably just rewatch something I know is good instead of finding something new.
