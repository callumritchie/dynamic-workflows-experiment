Interviewer: Thanks for making time. I'm curious about your experience with streaming — how do you typically decide what to watch?

P: Honestly, it's more chaotic than I'd like to admit. Last week my sister called me, and she wanted to watch The Bear together even though she's in Denver and I'm here in Portland. So I had to figure out if I had Hulu, which I do, but then I had to make sure we were on the same episode. It took like 15 minutes of texting back and forth before we actually started.

Interviewer: Walk me through that. You didn't immediately know if you had Hulu?

P: No, not really. I mean, I know I *have* it, but I couldn't remember if I'd cancelled it or not. There's so many subscriptions that I just... I have like six or seven of them, and I'm not totally sure which ones are actually active right now. I think I'm paying for something I don't use, but I can't remember which one.

Interviewer: How often does that happen — you're unsure about what you're subscribed to?

P: More than it should. Last month I went to watch something on HBO Max — or Max, whatever they call it now — and I couldn't get in. Turned out I wasn't subscribed anymore. I was pretty annoyed because I thought I was. Then I re-subscribed and watched one episode of something, and now I'm not sure if I'm still paying for it or if it auto-renewed. I should probably check my credit card statement, but that's tedious.

Interviewer: So when you're sitting down to watch something, how does that process usually go?

P: It depends. If I know what I want to watch, like if my mom texted me about a show everyone's talking about, I'll just scroll through my apps and see where it is. Sometimes I get lucky. But a lot of the time I end up clicking around three or four apps before I find it, or I don't find it at all and give up. Then I'll just watch something else that I know is on one of the apps.

Interviewer: That's frustrating. Do you remember a specific time recently when that happened?

P: Yeah, actually. Tuesday night, I wanted to watch that new season of Fallout because my roommate wouldn't stop talking about it. I was pretty sure it was on Prime, but I also thought maybe it was Apple TV? I spent like 10 minutes going back and forth, and then I texted him to ask, and he said it's on Prime. So I felt kind of dumb for not knowing. But the thing is, I pay for so many of these that it's hard to keep track of what's where.

Interviewer: Do you share your subscriptions with anyone?

P: Yeah, my parents watch some stuff on my Netflix account, and my sister has access to my Hulu. So sometimes I'll get angry that my password isn't working or something, and it turns out someone else is using it. Or my parents will start watching something and I can't figure out where it is because I'm not in their house, so they have to explain it to me. It's a whole thing.

Interviewer: What would make the whole "what am I subscribed to" piece easier?

P: I mean, honestly, I'd love to just... know. Like, have one place where I can see everything I'm paying for and what's on each thing. And maybe get a heads-up when something I might like is coming out. Right now I mostly find out about shows from my friends or Instagram. My sister will text me a screenshot of something and say "we should watch this," and then I have to hunt for it.

Interviewer: When you do find something together with someone who's not in your house, how does that usually work out?

P: We pick a show, and then we just agree to watch an episode or two at the same time and text about it. Or sometimes I'll watch ahead and then slow down when we actually sync up to watch together. It's nice, honestly. It makes watching stuff feel less lonely, especially since people moved away.

Interviewer: Does the cost of all these subscriptions ever come up in those conversations?

P: Not really. I mean, I don't think about it until I get my credit card bill and I'm like, "Wait, how much am I spending on this?" Then I feel guilty for like a day and think I should cancel something, but I never do because I'm worried I'll immediately need it.

Interviewer: Has that actually happened?

P: Not yet, but it could, right? That's why I keep them all. Although... okay, last year I cancelled Disney Plus for a few weeks, and then The Mandalorian came back or whatever, and I had to resubscribe. So now I just keep it.

Interviewer: You're paying for it even though you don't use it?

P: Probably. I haven't opened it in like two months. But I might in the next two weeks, so I'll keep paying.
